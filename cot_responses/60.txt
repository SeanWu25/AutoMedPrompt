Remote ischemic preconditioning is a procedure where brief periods of ischemia (lack of blood flow) are induced in a particular area of the body, with the aim of protecting other areas of the body from subsequent, more prolonged periods of ischemia. This procedure has been shown to be beneficial in reducing the risk of acute kidney injury (AKI) in certain clinical scenarios.
